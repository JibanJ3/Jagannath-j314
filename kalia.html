History of Lord Jagannath

Lord Jagannath is a very important form of Lord Vishnu, mainly worshipped in Puri, Odisha, India. According to history, the worship of Jagannath began many centuries ago and became widely known during the time of King Indradyumna, who is believed to have built the famous Jagannath Temple in Puri. The idol of Lord Jagannath, along with his brother Balabhadra and sister Subhadra, is unique because it is made of wood and is replaced every 12 to 19 years in a sacred ritual called “Nabakalebara.” The Jagannath Temple is one of the four holy Char Dham pilgrimage sites in India, and it is especially famous for the grand Rath Yatra festival, where the deities are taken out in huge chariots to give blessings to devotees. The tradition of Jagannath worship represents unity, devotion, and equality, as people from all backgrounds come together to serve the Lord.

Rath Yatra

Rath Yatra is one of the most famous festivals dedicated to Lord Jagannath, celebrated every year in Puri, Odisha. In this festival, Lord Jagannath, along with his elder brother Balabhadra and sister Subhadra, is taken out of the Jagannath Temple and placed on huge, beautifully decorated wooden chariots. These chariots are then pulled by thousands of devotees through the streets to the Gundicha Temple, which is considered the Lord’s garden house. The journey symbolizes Lord Jagannath coming out of the temple to give blessings to all people, without any discrimination. It is believed that even touching the ropes of the chariot brings great blessings and removes sins. Rath Yatra shows unity, devotion, and the idea that God belongs to everyone.

Temple Flags

The flag of the Jagannath Temple in Puri is very unique and considered sacred by devotees. It is placed on top of the temple’s main dome and is changed every day by a priest who climbs the temple without any safety equipment, which itself is seen as a divine tradition. One of the most interesting beliefs is that the flag always flutters in the opposite direction of the wind, which many people consider a spiritual mystery. The flag represents victory, protection, and the presence of Lord Jagannath in the temple. It is an important symbol of faith and devotion for millions of devotees visiting Puri.
